Afang Soup - Ingredients
  1. 2 cups waterleaf (chopped) or substitute spinach

  3. 1½ cups assorted meats (beef, tripe, kpomo) and/or smoked fish
  4. 1 cup stockfish or dried fish (optional)
  5. 2 tbsp ground crayfish
  6. 2 seasoning cubes (or to taste)
  7. Salt and ground pepper to taste
  8. 1½ – 2 cups palm oil

Afang Soup - Preparation
  1. Cook the assorted meats and stockfish in water with seasoning until tender; reserve the stock.
  2. Heat palm oil in a pot, add chopped onion and sauté briefly.
  3. Add a little of the meat stock, then the cooked meats, crayfish and pepper; stir and simmer 3–5 minutes.
  4. Add the waterleaf (or spinach) and cook 3–5 minutes until it softens.
  5. Stir in the shredded afang (okazi) leaveseduce heat and simmer 5–7 minutes until leaves are tender but not overcooked.
  6. Adjust seasoning and serve with fufu, pounded yam or eba.

Fried Rice - Ingredients
  1. 2 cups parboiled rice
  2. 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, green peas, sweet corn)
  3. 1 cup diced liver or cooked chicken
  4. 1 green bell pepper, diced
  5. 1 onion, chopped
  6. 2 cloves garlic, minced
  7. 1 tsp curry powder
  8. 1 tsp dried thyme
  9. 2 seasoning cubes (or to taste)
  10. Salt to taste
  11. 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  12. 1 cup chicken stock (or water)
  1. Fried Rice - Preparation
  2. Parboil the rice until just cooked, rinse under cold water and drain.
  3. Heat oil in a large pan; sauté chopped onion and garlic until translucent.
  4. Add diced liver or precooked chicken; stir-fry with curry and thyme for 2–3 minutes.
  5. Add mixed vegetables and cook briefly until slightly tender.
  6. Add the drained rice and a splash of chicken stock; fold gently to combine.
  7. Add diced green pepper, adjust salt/seasoning, and stir-fry on low heat 5–7 minutes until heated through.
  8. Serve hot.
  1. Jollof Rice - Ingredients
  2. 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  3. 4 large fresh tomatoes
  4. 2 red bell peppers
  5. 1 scotch bonnet (ata rodo) or to taste
  6. 1 onion (half for blending, half for frying)
  7. 2 tbsp tomato paste
  8. 3 seasoning cubes (or to taste)
  9. 1 tsp curry powder
  10. 1 tsp dried thyme
  11. 2 cups chicken stock (or water)
  12. 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  13. Salt to taste
  1. Jollof Rice - Preparation
  2. Blend the fresh tomatoes, red peppers and half the onion to a smooth paste.
  3. Heat oil in a pot, fry the remaining chopped onion briefly, add tomato paste and fry 2–3 minutes.
  4. Add the blended tomato mixture and cook, stirring occasionally, until the sauce reduces and oil begins to separate (15–20 minutes).
  5. Add seasoning cubes, curry, thyme and salt; stir in the washed rice.
  6. Add chicken stock (enough to cover rice by about 1 inch), stir, reduce heat, cover and simmer until rice is cooked (check and stir occasionally to avoid burning).
  7. When rice is done, fluff and serve with protein or plantain.
  1. Nigerian Stew - Ingredients
  2. 6 fresh tomatoes
  3. 2 red bell peppers
  4. 1 scotch bonnet (ata rodo), optional
  5. 1 onion (half for blending, half for frying)
  6. 2 tbsp tomato paste
  7. 500 g cooked or fried meat/chicken
  8. 3 tbsp vegetable oil (or palm oil mix)
  9. 2 seasoning cubes (or to taste)
  10. Salt and dried thyme to taste
  1. Nigerian Stew - Preparation
  2. Blend the fresh tomatoes, red peppers, scotch bonnet (if using) and half the onion into a smooth paste.
  3. Boil the blended mix briefly to reduce excess water (optional).
  4. Heat oil in a pot, fry the remaining chopped onion, then add tomato paste and fry 2–3 minutes.
  5. Add the blended tomato mixture and cook until the oil separates from the sauce (10–20 minutes), stirring occasionally.
  6. Add seasoning, thyme and the cooked/fried meat or chicken with a splash of stock; simmer 8–10 minutes to marry flavors.
  7. Serve hot with rice, yam or beans.
